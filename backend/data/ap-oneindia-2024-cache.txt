The Election Commission of India on Tuesday announced the results of the Andhra Pradesh assembly and Lok Sabha elections.

N Chandrababu Naidu, the national president of the Telugu Desam Party (TDP), is poised to assume office as the Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh for the fourth time.

The TDP defeated Jagan Mohan Reddy's YSR Congress Party in both the Assembly and Lok Sabha elections.

The TDP, now allied with the BJP-led National Democratic Alliance (NDA) along with the Jana Sena Party (JSP) of the state, clinched a decisive victory by securing 135 seats.

The JSP, under the leadership of Pawan Kalyan, significantly contributed with 21 seats, while the BJP emerged victorious in eight constituencies.
